# To-Stack-or-Diversify
This study analyzes whether stacking or diversification is a better fantasy football draft strategy using 2017-2019 NFL data. Simulated Best Ball teams and OLS models show which stacks are most beneficial in Half and Full PPR.

Numerous strategies are employed when assembling a fantasy football team, such as a heavy emphasis on running backs or securing a top-tier quarterback within the initial rounds of the draft. However, another strategic method frequently employed is player stacking, where individuals select players from the same NFL team, pairing a quarterback with a different player, as exemplified by combinations like Josh Allen and Stefon Diggs or Patrick Mahomes and Isiah Pacheco. The fundamental principle behind stacking is the correlation between the performance of the quarterback and the other player; when the quarterback excels, the other player is more likely to follow suit.
